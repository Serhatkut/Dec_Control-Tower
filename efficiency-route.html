Control Tower Overview & Alerts

This document defines the core operational KPIs for the DHL eCommerce Near Real-Time Control Tower. These metrics are designed to enable proactive management, allowing operators to identify and resolve issues before they impact the final service promise.

1. Primary Service Delivery KPIs (Outcome Focus)

These metrics represent the final "Product" delivered to the customer. In a Control Tower, these are your "North Star" metrics.

  • On-Time Successful Delivery (OTSD%): Percentage of parcels successfully delivered to the recipient or an authorized service point on or before the Estimated Delivery Date (EDD). (Control Tower Usage: Monitor actual delivery completions. If OTSD% drops while OTD% remains high, it indicates a "Last Mile Quality" issue)
    Strategic Importance: This is the ultimate measure of the customer experience. Unlike attempts, success is what the customer pays for.
  • On-Time Delivery Attempt (OTD%): Percentage of parcels where a delivery attempt was made on or before the EDD, regardless of whether the customer was home. (Control Tower Usage: Use live event triggers to see if drivers are hitting their route schedules)
    Strategic Importance: This measures our Service Promise. If we attempted the delivery on time but the customer wasn't there, we have fulfilled our operational SLA.

2. Lead Time & Phase Analytics (Process Focus)

These KPIs break down the Customer to Delivery Time (CTD-h) to pinpoint exactly where the "clock is bleeding."

  • Customer to Delivery Time (CTD-h): Total hours from first physical scan (or scheduled pickup) to successful delivery. (Control Tower Usage: Set "Pacing Alerts." e.g., > 18 hours on a 24-hour SLA triggers alert)
  • First Mile Average Hours (FMT-h): Pickup to Origin Hub/Service Center. (Usage: High FMT-h suggests pickup routes are over-capacity or origin hub receiving is bottlenecked)
  • Mid-Mile Average Hours (MMT-h): Origin Hub to Destination Hub/SC. (Usage: Monitor the "Dark Phase" to track linehaul performance and hub processing speed)
  • Last Mile Average Hours (LMT-h): Destination Hub/SC to Delivery. (Usage: High LMT-h often points to "Daily Turnover" or "Backlog" at the local delivery depot)

3. Network Health & Exception Metrics (Diagnostic Focus)

These metrics identify systemic risks that cause delays in the lead times mentioned above.

  • Network On-Time Performance (NOP%): Percentage of parcels reaching the destination node (Hub/SC) within the EDD. (Usage: This is your early warning system. Prioritize at-risk parcels)
  • Linehaul Utilization (LU%): Percentage of vehicle capacity used during transit. (Usage: Delay departure if low; trigger Ad-hoc Trucks if 95-100% capacity and pending volume remains)
  • Backlog Ratio (BLG%): Percentage of parcels sitting in a facility beyond their EDD. (Usage: Monitor Age of Inventory. BLG% > 5% requires immediate labor injection)
  • Damage (DR%) & Loss (LR%) Ratios: Percentage of parcels flagged with damage/loss scans relative to total handled. (Usage: Identify "Hot Spots" like sorting belts where damage scans spike)

4. Efficiency & Productivity KPIs

These metrics track how efficiently our assets (vehicles and people) are being used during the live shift.

  • Stops per Route (SPR): Total number of stops completed divided by the total number of routes. (Usage: Monitor route productivity. Low SPR suggests poor route density)
  • Stops per On-Route Hour (SPOR): Total stops divided by the number of active hours spent on the route. (Usage: Real-time driver efficiency. Drops trigger localized investigations)
  • Pieces per Hour (PPH): Total number of parcels processed divided by the total labor hours worked. (Usage: Monitor facility throughput. Drops flag processing bottlenecks)

6. How to Use the Control Tower Dashboard

CadenceAction
HourlyCheck BLG%, FMT-h, and PPH. Are we clearing the hub floor efficiently?
Mid-ShiftMonitor NOP%, LU%, and SPOR. Are trucks full? Are drivers moving at expected pace? Request Ad-hoc trucks if overflow exists.
End-of-DayReview OTSD% vs OTD%. Did we make the attempts? Analyze the Exceptions List to identify primary drivers of failure.
What is Important?
  • Event-Driven Data: Only use real-time timestamps (First Scan, Hub Scan, Out-for-Delivery). Do not rely on batch uploads.
  • The Golden Layer: All measurements must come from the tbl_master_shipment_e2e to ensure the "Single Point of Truth."
  • Proactive vs. Reactive: A Control Tower should not tell you what happened (Yesterday's OTD), it should tell you what is about to happen (Current NOP% and Backlog).
